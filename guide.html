Introduction into Aion 3.9

What is Aion?

Aion is a fantasy-themed MMORPG (Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game) set in the shattered world of Atreia, where two powerful factions — the radiant Elyos and the shadowed Asmodians — battle for survival and dominance. Unlike many MMOs, Aion stands out with its breathtaking environments, rich lore, and the unique ability to spread your wings and take to the skies in aerial combat.

Released in the golden era of MMORPGs, Aion quickly gained a reputation for its beautiful art style, large-scale PvP battles, and deep character progression. Whether you’re diving into epic dungeons, engaging in fierce abyss sieges, or simply exploring its stunning landscapes, Aion offers a mix of challenge and adventure that keeps you hooked.

Aion 3.9 in particular is remembered as one of the most balanced and community-driven patches, where teamwork, strategy, and skill mattered more than anything else. It’s a world where every decision counts — from choosing your class and stigma build, to how you fight in the skies above Atreia.

What Classes Are Available in Aion 3.9?

In Aion 3.9, there are 8 main classes to choose from, each with its own unique playstyle and role in both PvE and PvP. Your class choice not only defines how you fight but also how you experience the world of Atreia. At level 10, your character ascends and branches into a specialized path, giving you access to powerful new skills and abilities.

Warrior – Strong melee fighters who excel at close combat.

  • Gladiator: Masters of two-handed weapons and polearms, Gladiators deal devastating area damage and excel at controlling groups of enemies.
  • Templar: The tanks of Aion, Templars use heavy armor and shields to protect their allies while holding enemy attention.

Scout – Agile and fast, Scouts specialize in precision and mobility.

  • Ranger: Long-ranged bow users who strike from a distance with deadly accuracy, perfect for kiting enemies.
  • Assassin: Masters of stealth and daggers, Assassins deal explosive burst damage but require quick reflexes and precision.

Mage – Powerful spellcasters who wield destructive magic.

  • Sorcerer: The ultimate glass cannon, Sorcerers unleash massive damage with elemental spells but are fragile if caught.
  • Spiritmaster: Summoners who command elemental spirits to fight alongside them, controlling the battlefield with debuffs and crowd control.

Priest – Supportive and versatile, Priests are vital to any group.

  • Cleric: The main healers of Aion, Clerics keep allies alive with powerful heals and protective buffs, while wearing chain armor for durability.
  • Chanter: A hybrid class that provides team-wide buffs (mantras), healing, and solid melee combat, making them essential in both PvE and PvP.

These eight classes form the heart of Aion’s combat system. Whether you want to crush enemies head-on, snipe from afar, command magical forces, or support your team, there’s a playstyle for every kind of adventurer in Atreia.

Elyos Levelling Guide

  • Lv1 to Lv10 Kill monsters in Poeta while moving to Pernos
  • Lv10 to Lv15 Kill monsters in Verteron
  • Lv15 to Lv21 Kill Verteron Kralls (at Ardus Shrine Entrance)
  • Lv21 to Lv25 Kill monsters in Novan's Crossing
  • Lv25 to Lv30 Nochsana Training Camp
  • Lv30 to Lv35 Little campaigns + Fire Temple Solo/duo (your choice)
  • Lv35 to Lv46 Go to Aetherogenic Lab + Campaign Quests
  • Lv46 to Lv53 Go to Adma Stronghold
  • Lv48 to Lv53 Alternative - Go to Dark Poeta (Clean all monsters) Solo (if Magical), Duo (if Physical)
  • Lv53 to Lv60 Go to Beshmundir Temple (Easy Mode) Solo (if Magical), Duo (if Physical)
  • Lv53 to Lv60 Alternative - Stay in Dark Poeta to farm money & complete Sarpan/Tiamaranta Campaign Quests

Asmodian Level Guide

  • Lv1 to Lv9 Kill random monsters while moving to Munin (Class Change Quest)
  • Lv10 to Lv25 Hit mobs in Altgard until Lv16, then go to Haramel
  • Lv25 to Lv30 Nochsana Training Camp
  • Lv30 to Lv35–40 Do small campaigns + Fire Temple (solo/duo) or Alquimia Research Lab
  • Lv35–40 to Lv46 Grind campaigns (easy ones) — ensure enough Tribunus tokens; do Brusthonin quests after 45+
  • Lv46 to Lv48–50 Go to Theobomos Lab (clean main + left rooms), exit and repeat
  • Lv48–50 to Lv53 Go to Dark Poeta (clear all mobs) — solo (if magical), duo (if physical)
  • Lv53 to Lv60 Go to Beshmundir Temple (easy mode) — solo (if magical), duo (if physical)
  • Lv53 to Lv60 Alternative — stay in Dark Poeta to farm kinah and finish Sarpan/Tiamaranta campaigns

Starter Gear

What is Starter Gear?

Starter Gear is a special set of 3 weapons and 1 armor set designed to help you level up quickly. It’s equivalent in stats to level 50 Daevanion gear, making it strong for early and mid-game content.

NOTE: The gear can't be socketted or enchanted, but you can socket and enchant weapon

Where to get it?

You'll receive the full set by completing the “A Ceremony in Pandaemonium” (Asmo) or “A Ceremony in Sanctum” (Elyos) quest — right after Ascension!

What is Atreia Pass?

  • The Atreia Pass is a non-pay-to-win (non-P2W) loyalty system. By completing missions and running instances, players earn tokens that allow them to progress through the pass.
  • These tokens can be exchanged on the official website for exclusive rewards.
  • Rewards include cosmetics, consumables, and character-enhancing items.
  • The system is designed to keep players engaged by offering valuable bonuses in return for their time and dedication.

Ways to obtain Atreia Pass Tokens

  • Staying online — For every hour you spend in-game, you earn 1 token.
  • Completing Campaign Quests — Finish yellow campaign quests to receive additional tokens (one time only).
  • Defeating Final Bosses — Earn tokens by killing final bosses in endgame instances and open-world content.
  • Doing Daily & Weekly Quests — Tokens are also rewarded for completing daily and weekly quests, as well as certain normal (blue) quests.
  • → You can find the full list of quests that give tokens here.

Exchanging Atreia Pass Tokens

  • Log out from the game (Character Selection works too).
  • Go to the Elden Aion Website and log in.
  • Click on "Atreia Pass".
  • Select your character name.
  • Click on "Exchange Tokens".
  • Based on the amount exchanged, click the bonuses — they will be delivered via Express Mail in-game.

What are Prism Cores?

  • Prism Core is a special currency earned primarily by completing campaign (yellow) quests.
  • The number of Cores you receive increases as you progress through higher-level campaign content.
  • These Cores can be exchanged for Fleet Commander's Equipment Boxes — a full Level 50 PvP gear set (E50) — through specific NPCs.

About Spiritmaster:

What can I expect from playing a Spiritmaster in Ethereal Aion? Quick reflexes at the start of the battle, multitasking, and positioning strategy is essential to mastering the Spiritmaster class. If you are caught with your robes down, failure to quickly cast a shield or a Spirit Substitution spell might result in a quick defeat.

You will need to keep in mind that anything getting through the defense you will build up with debuffs and summons will cause you a lot of damage. Multitasking between you and your summon will be essential to the effective use of the class, requiring you to pay attention to the proper positioning as well as proper timing for actions involving both of the units.

Unique abilities of Spiritmaster:

Spirit Erosion skill increases the drop rate of rare items.

Unique ability to remove some of the buffs from bosses in higher-level instance areas.

Beyond all of that, Spiritmasters are still great secondary DPS dealers and are useful debuffers.
